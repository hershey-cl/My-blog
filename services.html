Every port is a dreamer



Poems are the reflections of their authors because they always hold emotions and dreams. Writing them makes me feel better because I can express my feelings and opinions. Whenever I get pressured by the school tasks and from my family, I write poems that will help me release my frustration. Sometimes I write poems about specific people, which is extremely rare. These poems usually take a lot of my time because I put Easter eggs on them.





Here are two of my works :




"In the absence of light"






In the Absence of Light is the fourth poem of the Man's All Stages, an anthology written by me. The idea revolves around the lover stage of man, and I dedicate it to an important person.
"You can see the universe in someone's eyes," Irene Greenway said. In the poem, I described his eyes and what it does. The affection is evident because I poured my emotions into it. I am grateful for this person because somehow he saved me by helping me to be better







"Dear Hypocrites"






Dear Hypocrites is a poem/open letter for people who judge others negatively but act like they are saints.
"I heard people singing and delivering the word of God on my mother's phone, and it inspired me to write about people who go to church for show. I remembered some people who discriminate against the LGBTQIA++ community. I remembered some who judge others through their appearance and some through their past mistakes.



Society is weird, and poetry can help cure it.
